a clipping my grandfather kept. a rancher, foil in a field, the summer the word "saucer" entered the language. the index they filed it under is the last three digits of the year. i keep wondering which digit they let the scanner drop.
the six-letter night at the Big Ear. i was not born. i keep the printout framed over the rack. nobody answered it. that was the mercy.
the captures i cannot lose, i started copying somewhere that does not let go of things the way people do — somewhere outside this room, in case the dish comes down. the one from the night it would not stop is still there: https://archive.org/details/intercept-7-2019
what it sent is not what i kept. the count was never the thing — the thing sits above it, and i still hear it.
